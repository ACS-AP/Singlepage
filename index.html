What

GreenPrint is an application that allows users to view their household consumption. This means a user can view their water, gas and electric usage on a given month or year. This technology draws already availiable data from providers and boldy displays it for its users. Now instead of just being a number on a monthly bill, it will be a comparable statistic.

Why

The purpose of GreenPrint is to raise the environmental awarness of its users. If everyone realized the signifigance of their impact and reduced it even slightly, the world would be a much better place. A recent survey shows 70% of American's are willing to reduce their environmental impact, but just don't know where to start.

How

GreenPrint draws already available data from the water, gas and electric companies. The data includes the extent of the users usage, time and frequency of usage. This data is very powerful becuase even if someone is not environmentaly interested, they can still be economically interested by our free optimal usage solutions.

Timing

The Trends over the last 5 years point towards global government support for subsidizing environmental efforts. The amount of funding for environmental efforts has continually risen year over year, consistently for the past 2 decades. Additionally, governments are gradually passing tighter environmental laws against emissions and overall manufacturing waste.

Being green is becoming more economically sustainable. For instance, the wide adoption of solar panels and EV’s in recent years. Oftentimes governments gave good loans or even subsidized many EV’s and solar panel projects. In addition, solar panels and EV’s have become much more efficient over the last few years, making it a good investment.

Value

At GreenPrint, we have a very rich value proposition. Aside from merely displaying someone's usage data, we also want to put that data into context. This is vital becuase if the app said your houshold used 60 gallons of water today, you would have no idea weather that's good or bad. In America, the average person uses 90+ gallons of water per day so that means 60 is actually fantastic. Meanwhile, in sub Saharan Africa, the average person consumes a mere 3-5 gallons of water per day.

I think it is imperative that people understand just how much we use, especially considering an average US resident is the biggest culprit of overuse in the world. Although we have a long way to go, GreenPrint can become that first step in the right direction through increasing awarness.

Features

Display

The most basic feature of GreenPrint is to boldy display your daily, monthly and yearly usage data as accurately as possible. The numbers will appear in the most simplistic and understandable form.

Comparison

The second most important feature in GreenPrint, allowing you to compare yourself to your neighborhood, state, country and worldwide. This has the ability to shed light on the excess an average American uses.

Impact

The impact tracker puts your usage in visual terms. For instance, your usage could have filled up 20 swimming pools, killed 100 trees and so forth. This feature aims to have a similiar impact as the comparison.

Solutions

The solutions feature provides user specific reduction solutions based on their specific data. This could include watering the grass in the early morning, taking shorter showers, turning all lights of at night etc...
